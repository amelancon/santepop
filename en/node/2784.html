These awards aim to acknowledge students' contribution to the field of population health research. The prize will be awarded to Master's and Ph. D. students registered in a Quebec university and who have been distinguished by the quality of their research.

Click on the "Applications" tab above for more information
Value
  • Master's: $500
  • Ph. D.: $1000

Next submission deadline: to be announced

Contact and applications

genevieve.malboeuf@teluq.ca

 

The RRSPQ is pleased to announce its competition for Master’s (MSc) and Doctorate’s (PhD) students. 

The awards recognize the students’ contribution to the field of Population Health Research.

Eligibility

  • Master's and PhD theses must have been defended and accepted during the XXXX-XXXX (n/a) academic year in Quebec (i.e.,  before TE BE ANNOUNCED).
  • Students who are not members of the RRSPQ can also apply.
  • Master and Doctoral theses in all disciplines related to public health and populations are eligible

Application file:

  • Application form
  • Resume (CV)
  • Academic transcripts (unofficial copy accepted)
  • Copy of the thesis (a dropbox link (or alike) to the master's or thesis can be used instead of attaching heavy files in your application email)
  • A 500-word text detailing the thesis' contribution to Population Health Research.
  • For the PhD Thesis: a letter of support from the jury's external examinator for the evaluation of the thesis must be sent by the examinator to genevieve.malboeuf@teluq.ca
  • For the masters'thesis: a letter of support from the member of the jury for the evaluation of the masters'thesis (other than the director) must be sent by the examinator to genevieve.malboeuf@teluq.ca

Send you application to: genevieve.malboeuf@teluq.ca

Commitments:

  • Recipients must be present at the award ceremony which will take place at the QPHRN Scientific Day or General Assembly (unless otherwise stated). Recipients could be asked to present their thesis during these events. Depending on the circumstances, a short video presentation – that would be published on the RRSPQ website - could be requested.
  • Awarded students agree to post an abstract of their thesis as well as the link to their thesis on the QPHRN website

Selection Committee:

The selection committee will be composed of researchers and decision-makers in the field of Population Health Research.

Selection criteria:

  • Content's quality and originality
  • Relevance to Population Health Research
  • Congruity with the mission, goals and strategic priorities of the Network
  • Ability to support the advancement of reflection and research in population health, impact of the research and knowledge transfer
  • Impact on Population Health
  • Multidisciplinarity
  • Quality of academic file
Value
  • Master's: $500
  • Ph. D.: $1000
